Visualizations: Latitude VS Temperature
Is this creepy??

Let's talk about it

This beautiful scatter plot shows depicts the increase in temperature as one moves closer to the Equator. On the x-axis, we have the latitude of cities around the world. As we go North of the Equator, the latitudes are positive. As we go south of the Equator, the latitudes are negative. On the y-axis, we have the maximum temperature of the city throughout the year.
This chart clearly shows a trend in increased temperatures as one moves closer to the Equator. Interestingly, there are a few cities SOUTH of the Equator that had higher temperatures than at the Equator itself. If this data scientist had to guess, that's probably because the Earth is not only round (If you're a flat-earther, you can just close your browser now), but it is also tilted slightly. Just ever. So. Slightly. such that the cities JUST South of the Equator may get a bit more heat during parts of the year.
