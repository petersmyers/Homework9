Let's talk about it

We often associate heat with humidity. As this data scientist lives in St Louis, the summers are terrible, not because of the heat, but because of the humidity. Swimming through a swamp of heavy air is unpleasant at best and not something that draw tourists to come along down to the banks of the Mississippi. Interestingly, this graph tells us that you have a relatively even probability of suffering from humidity no matter how close you are to the Equator.
This scatter plot has latitude on the x-axis, denoting how close a given city may be to the Equator. On the y-axis, we have the % humidity on average for said city. We can see that no clear trend exists in this data that would suggest that closer to the Equator means higher humidity. If such a trend existed, then we would expect to see something akin to higher humidity in the middle of the graph, centered around the Equator, and lower humidity on either side.
